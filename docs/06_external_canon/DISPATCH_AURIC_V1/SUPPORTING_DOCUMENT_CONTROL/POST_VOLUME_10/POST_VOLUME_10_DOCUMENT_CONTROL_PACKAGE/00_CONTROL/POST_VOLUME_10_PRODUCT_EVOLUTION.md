# POST–VOLUME 10 PRODUCT EVOLUTION

## Executive Finding

The post–Volume 10 work represents a clear shift from constitutional platform architecture toward operational intelligence: domain ecosystems, truth models, canonical objects, workflows, agents, connectors, profiles, relationship graphs, evidence rules, readiness scoring, and implementation handoff material. The historical roadmap explicitly described this shift as encoding business knowledge rather than adding more infrastructure.

## What Changed After Volume X

1. **Domain breadth expanded.** The source estate moves beyond universal architecture into financial services, credit unions, fintech, capital markets, lending, payments, governance, risk, and institutional operations.
2. **Objects became operational.** Institutions, vendors, executives, committees, products, systems, pilots, investments, evidence, approvals, and relationships are represented as reusable operating objects.
3. **Lifecycle thinking became explicit.** Buying, onboarding, vendor review, pilot execution, lending, innovation, investment committee, regulatory change, and publication workflows appear as repeatable operating sequences.
4. **The graph became central.** Institution, executive, vendor, product, system, regulator, investor, and relationship profiles are treated as a living intelligence graph rather than directory records.
5. **Evidence and confidence entered the architecture.** Source quality, freshness, confidence, and provenance became first-class constraints.
6. **Commercial and implementation pathways emerged.** Pilot readiness, institution matching, public profiles, private Terminal onboarding, CUSO partnerships, publication, and build handoff material connect the architecture to deployment.

## Relationship to FS-4000 Through FS-14000

- **FS-4000:** ecosystems, domain ontologies, truth models, operating rules, and cartridge knowledge.
- **FS-5000:** object, relationship, agent, domain, and connector registries.
- **FS-6000:** institution profiles, readiness, opportunity, evidence, approval, and lifecycle inputs.

- **FS-9000:** market graph, profile schemas, institution population, and relationship models.
- **FS-10000:** public profile and private Terminal concepts, dashboards, and institution-facing surfaces.
- **FS-11000:** workflow and agent catalogs, operating triggers, and runtime inputs.
- **FS-12000:** risk, compliance, evidence, confidence, governance, and human authority constraints.
- **FS-13000:** pilot, onboarding, matching, partnership, publication, and commercialization pathways.
- **FS-14000:** acceptance tests, implementation backlog, handoff, and validation inputs.

## Material Before or Outside the Current FS Structure

The constitutional relationship among Dispatch, Auric, the Terminal, the Cooperative Markets concept, cartridges, SDK/extension philosophy, publication/network strategy, and managed operating model may belong partly in foundational product layers preceding FS-4000. This audit does not create FS-1000–FS-3000; it records the unresolved need for the later Volumes I–X comparison.

## Historical Roadmap Interpretation

The roadmap percentages are retained as historical evidence of the project’s perceived gaps immediately after Volumes I–X. They are not current maturity scores. Later work and the completed FS packages materially changed the state of the documentation estate.

## Net Product Evolution

The product evolved from an AI-enabled operating-system constitution into a specification for a managed institutional intelligence environment capable of understanding institutions, populating a graph, routing governed work, coordinating specialized agents, preserving evidence, and enabling pilots, partnerships, investments, and institutional operating workflows.
