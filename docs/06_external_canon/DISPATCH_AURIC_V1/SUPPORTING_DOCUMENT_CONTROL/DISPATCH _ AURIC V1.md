# **3\. PRODUCT NAMES AND RELATIONSHIP**

## **Dispatch**

* ingesting business and institutional context  
* creating canonical operating objects

# **4\. THE MARKET PROBLEM**

Financial institutions and operating companies do not suffer primarily from a lack of software.

They suffer from fragmentation.

Important information is distributed across:

* email  
* spreadsheets  
* shared drives  
* regulatory filings  
* board materials  
* vendor systems  
* core systems  
* CRMs  
* project tools  
* reports  
* contracts  
* employee knowledge  
* consultants  
* disconnected SaaS applications

The institution may possess the information required to make a decision, but it cannot reliably assemble, interpret, govern, and act on that information.

Typical institutional problems include:

* incomplete or stale profiles  
* poor visibility into vendor and system dependencies  
* unclear decision authority  
* weak evidence management  
* manual regulatory response  
* slow product and vendor review  
* fragmented board reporting  
* incomplete fintech readiness  
* missed revenue opportunities  
* no coordinated institutional memory  
* AI usage without governance  
* excessive dependence on individual employees  
* no reliable path from information to execution

Most AI tools address isolated tasks.

They summarize a document, answer a question, generate an email, search a data source, or automate a narrow process.

They do not create a governed operating system for the institution.

# **5\. THE PRODUCT THESIS**

Dispatch turns fragmented institutional context into a governed operating system.

The system should be able to:

1. Understand the institution.  
2. Understand the people, products, vendors, systems, regulations, risks, and relationships around it.  
3. Determine what the institution is trying to accomplish.  
4. Detect opportunities and problems.  
5. Select the correct workflow.  
6. Assign the correct specialized agents.  
7. Retrieve the required evidence.  
8. Enforce human approvals.  
9. Produce the correct institutional output.  
10. Preserve the audit trail.  
11. Measure the result.  
12. Improve the institutional profile and future decisions.

The simplest product framing is:

## **Understand**

Build the institutional digital twin.

## **Decide**

Identify priorities, risks, readiness, and opportunities.

## **Execute**

Route governed workflows using agents, tools, data, and human operators.

## **Prove**

Preserve evidence, approval, lineage, versioning, and audit history.

# **7\. CORE PRODUCT PRINCIPLES**

## **Managed, Not Merely Licensed**

The customer should not be forced to become an AI infrastructure company.

Auric Works may configure, host, operate, and improve the environment.

## **Private and Permissioned**

Institutional data remains governed by:

* tenant  
* role  
* object  
* field  
* evidence class  
* workflow  
* approval authority  
* data classification  
* model-use policy

## **Human Authority Remains Intact**

Agents may:

* retrieve  
* classify  
* summarize  
* calculate  
* compare  
* draft  
* recommend  
* route  
* monitor  
* detect opportunities  
* assemble evidence

Agents may not independently:

* approve loans  
* approve investments  
* commit capital  
* execute trades  
* bind insurance  
* issue final legal opinions  
* issue final compliance determinations  
* communicate with regulators  
* approve vendors  
* waive policy  
* make final suitability determinations

## **Evidence Before Assertion**

Material conclusions should link to:

* source  
* date  
* owner  
* confidence  
* freshness  
* transformation  
* review status  
* approval status

## **Public, Private, Attested, and Inferred Data Stay Separate**

The system must not flatten these into an indistinguishable profile.

## **Missing Information Must Be Visible**

Unknown is not the same as false.

Stale is not the same as current.

Inferred is not the same as verified.

Draft is not the same as approved.

## **The Network Is a Byproduct of Operational Value**

The primary product is institutional intelligence and execution.

Deal flow, referrals, pilots, partnerships, investments, and capital-market activity emerge as a byproduct of the operating network.
