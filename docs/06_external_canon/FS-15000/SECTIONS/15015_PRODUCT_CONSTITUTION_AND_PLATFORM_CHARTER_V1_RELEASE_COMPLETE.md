**Version:** 1.0

# 1. Purpose

This charter establishes the constitutional principles that govern the market-facing Dispatch / Auric platform.

It exists to prevent product drift, preserve institutional trust, protect human authority, maintain editorial integrity, separate public record from private operating context, constrain commercialization, and ensure that every future product decision remains consistent with the controlling product hierarchy:

1. **The Auric** is the public intelligence and publication-of-record layer.
2. **Named Terminals** are the institution-facing product and operating surface.
3. **Dispatch OS** is the underlying governed operating architecture.
4. **Cartridges, registries, connectors, workflows, agents, evidence, and approvals** are implementation mechanisms, not separate products competing for authority.

This document is a constitutional specification. It does not duplicate runtime, UI, security, connector, or deployment specifications. It defines the principles those specifications must obey.

---

# 2. Constitutional Product Thesis

Auric Works is building a governed institutional operating environment that converts fragmented information into trusted institutional intelligence and controlled execution.

The platform must:

- understand institutions, people, products, vendors, systems, risks, regulations, opportunities, and relationships;
- distinguish public, private, synthetic, attested, inferred, and agent-generated information;
- route work through governed workflows;
- preserve human authority over regulated or material decisions;
- turn publication and market intelligence into durable institutional profiles;
- turn profiles into readiness, matching, engagement, implementation, and verified outcomes;
- use verified outcomes to improve future intelligence;
- remain useful before any transaction, referral, investment, sale, or implementation occurs.

The network is a byproduct of operational value. It is not permission to convert trust into undisclosed commercial preference.

# 4. Constitutional Definitions

## 4.1 Public Record

Information lawfully available for public use and represented with source, date, provenance, confidence, and freshness.

## 4.2 Claimed Profile

A public-facing profile for which an authorized representative has asserted control and supplied attested information. Claiming does not convert unverified assertions into verified facts.

## 4.3 Verified Profile

A profile whose material fields have passed the applicable source, attestation, evidence, and review requirements.

## 4.4 Private Operating Context

Tenant-controlled information used inside a Named Terminal or Dispatch workflow. It is not public merely because the platform also maintains a public profile of the same institution or company.

## 4.5 Named Terminal

The branded institutional operating product configured for a specific institution, company, network, or approved operator.

## 4.6 Governed Engagement

A controlled interaction among institutions, companies, vendors, partners, investors, or other authorized parties in which eligibility, purpose, consent, evidence, authority, disclosures, and outcomes are preserved.

## 4.7 Material Action

An action that may commit capital, bind an institution, approve or reject a regulated matter, create legal exposure, change a policy, authorize access, publish a material assertion, communicate on behalf of an institution, or materially affect a person or counterparty.

## 4.8 Human Authority

The power held by an authorized human decision-maker to approve, reject, amend, escalate, suspend, or close a material action.

## 4.9 Institutional Trust

The measurable condition created when information, authority, provenance, conflicts, evidence, and decisions are visible and reliably governed.

# 5. Immutable Constitutional Principles

The following principles are immutable for V1 and may be changed only through the amendment process in this charter.

## 5.1 One-Product Rule

The market-facing system must be experienced as one coherent product, not as a loose collection of publication, CRM, marketplace, chatbot, data room, and workflow tools.

## 5.2 Public and Private Separation

Public record, claimed information, verified information, private tenant data, inferred information, synthetic data, and agent-generated outputs must remain distinguishable in storage, retrieval, display, export, and decision support.

## 5.3 Evidence Before Assertion

Material assertions require provenance, source, date, owner, confidence, freshness, transformation history, and review status.

## 5.4 Unknown Is Explicit

Unknown is not false. Stale is not current. Inferred is not verified. Draft is not approved. Missing evidence cannot be silently replaced with model confidence.

## 5.5 Human Authority Is Preserved

Agents may retrieve, classify, compare, calculate, summarize, recommend, draft, route, monitor, and assemble evidence. Agents may not independently exercise prohibited decision authority.

## 5.6 Editorial Truth Is Not for Sale

Payment, sponsorship, advertising, investment, referral economics, implementation work, customer status, or partnership status may not purchase favorable editorial treatment, ranking, verification, or eligibility.

## 5.7 Commercial Relationships Are Disclosed

Material commercial, investment, referral, ownership, sponsorship, or implementation relationships must be disclosed wherever they could reasonably affect interpretation.

## 5.8 Matching Is Governed

Matching may consider eligibility, readiness, fit, permissions, evidence, and stated preferences. It may not secretly prefer the highest payer.

## 5.9 Institutional Consent Governs Private Use

Private data may be used only for approved purposes, by authorized actors, within applicable retention, model-use, and disclosure boundaries.

## 5.10 The Network Must Produce Operational Value

A participant must receive meaningful intelligence, readiness, workflow, or institutional value independent of whether a transaction occurs.

# 6. Rights of Institutional Participants

Every institution, company, authorized user, and approved counterparty has the right to:

- know whether information is public, private, attested, inferred, synthetic, or agent-generated;
- understand the material sources supporting a conclusion;
- see whether information is stale, disputed, incomplete, or under review;
- know when commercial relationships or conflicts are relevant;
- control private institutional data subject to law and contract;
- review and correct claimed-profile information;
- receive an explanation of material matching or readiness outcomes;
- require human review of material automated recommendations;
- appeal or dispute a material profile, eligibility, ranking, or workflow determination;
- know which organization and human authority made a final decision;
- obtain an auditable record of approvals, evidence, and material changes;
- exit a commercial relationship without erasing public truth or required audit history.

These rights are subject to legitimate confidentiality, security, legal privilege, regulatory, and third-party restrictions.

A Named Terminal owner does not obtain editorial control over The Auric merely by becoming a customer.

# 9. Editorial Constitution

## 9.1 Publication Authority

The Auric must serve as a trusted record of institutional intelligence, not as promotional inventory for commercial relationships.

## 9.2 Separation of Functions

Editorial selection, commercial sales, matching, profile verification, investment activity, referral activity, and implementation services must have distinct decision authorities and auditable handoffs.

## 9.3 Corrections

Material errors must be corrected through a recorded correction lifecycle. Corrections must preserve what changed, why, when, by whom, and based on what evidence.

## 9.4 Right of Response

Subjects of material coverage must have a reasonable right to respond where fairness, context, or dispute resolution requires it. A right of response is not a right to suppress accurate reporting.

## 9.5 Sponsored Content

Sponsored content must be conspicuously labeled and excluded from independent editorial rankings unless the methodology explicitly allows inclusion without paid preference.

## 9.6 Rankings and Methodologies

Material rankings, scores, awards, lists, and readiness assessments must disclose methodology, scope, material exclusions, update cadence, and relevant conflicts.

# 10. Commercial Constitution

Commercial activity may include platform fees, implementation fees, managed services, usage/pass-through charges, sponsorship, advertising, subscriptions, referral economics, transaction or execution revenue, and strategic partnerships.

Commercial activity may not:

- purchase verification;
- purchase editorial conclusions;
- suppress truthful public information;
- create undisclosed matching preference;
- bypass eligibility, evidence, approval, or conflict review;
- authorize an agent to make a regulated final decision;
- convert private institutional data into public or commercial inventory without authority;
- misrepresent a referral as independent advice;
- misrepresent an implementation service as a regulatory approval;
- condition access to a correction process on payment.

All material commercial pathways must identify the payer, beneficiary, service, trigger, economic model, disclosures, approval requirements, and prohibited uses.

# 11. Human Authority Constitution

## 11.1 Prohibited Independent Agent Actions

No agent may independently:

- approve, decline, or condition a loan;
- approve an investment or commit capital;
- execute a trade or securities transaction;
- bind insurance;
- approve a vendor or waive vendor-risk requirements;
- issue a final legal opinion;
- issue a final compliance determination;
- make a final suitability, accreditation, or fiduciary determination;
- communicate with a regulator as the institution without approval;
- alter an approved policy;
- disclose private data to an external party;
- publish a material allegation without editorial approval;
- create or revoke privileged access without authorized approval.

## 11.2 Required Approval Attributes

Every material approval must record:

- approval ID;
- decision type;
- authorized role;
- named decision-maker;
- scope of authority;
- evidence reviewed;
- conflicts and recusals;
- decision;
- conditions;
- timestamp;
- effective period;
- version;
- reason or rationale;
- appeal or escalation path.

## 11.3 Emergency Suspension

Auric Works and authorized tenant administrators may suspend an agent, workflow, connector, model route, or publication process when security, privacy, authority, evidence, or integrity controls fail.

# 12. Data and Knowledge Constitution

## 12.1 Canonical Provenance Classes

Every material data element must support one of the following provenance classes:

| Class | Meaning | May Be Treated as Verified? |
|---|---|---:|
| Public | Lawfully obtained public information | Only after source validation |
| Private | Tenant-controlled nonpublic information | Only within authorized scope |
| Attested | Asserted by an authorized party | Not automatically |
| Verified | Supported by required evidence and review | Yes, within scope and freshness |
| Inferred | Derived from rules, models, or relationships | No |
| Synthetic | Created for testing, demonstration, or simulation | No |
| Agent-generated | Produced by an AI or automated process | No, until reviewed |

## 12.2 Required Knowledge Attributes

A material knowledge object must carry:

- object ID;
- source ID;
- source type;
- provenance class;
- tenant and visibility;
- owner;
- effective date;
- observed date;
- freshness status;
- confidence;
- verification status;
- transformation lineage;
- model involvement;
- dispute status;
- retention class;
- access classification.

## 12.3 No Silent Flattening

The platform must not flatten conflicting, stale, inferred, disputed, private, and public assertions into one undifferentiated field.

# 13. Profile Constitution

Public, claimed, verified, and private profiles are distinct layers.

A claimed profile grants the authorized claimant the ability to submit, attest, clarify, and dispute information. It does not grant the ability to rewrite public history or hide accurate public information.

A verified badge or equivalent status must be scoped. Verification may apply to identity, authority, a specific field, a document, a capability, or a time period. It must not imply blanket endorsement.

Profile changes must preserve:

- previous value;
- new value;
- source;
- actor;
- reason;
- review state;
- effective date;
- visibility effect;
- dispute status.

---

# 14. Matching and Opportunity Constitution

Matching exists to identify eligible, relevant, and permissioned opportunities.

Every material match must support an explanation containing:

- matched entities;
- use case;
- eligibility rules;
- matched criteria;
- unmet criteria;
- disqualifiers;
- evidence;
- confidence;
- freshness;
- conflict disclosures;
- commercial influence, if any;
- next permitted action;
- required human approval.

A match is not an endorsement, approval, suitability determination, investment recommendation, or promise of implementation.

Commercial weighting must be either prohibited or explicitly disclosed and separated from neutral relevance scoring.

---

# 15. Governed Engagement Constitution

No material external engagement may proceed solely because a match exists.

The engagement lifecycle must include, as applicable:

1. purpose definition;
2. participant eligibility;
3. consent and authority;
4. conflict review;
5. disclosure delivery;
6. evidence sufficiency;
7. data-sharing scope;
8. authorized communication channel;
9. meeting or diligence workflow;
10. decision authority;
11. implementation or transaction controls;
12. outcome capture;
13. post-engagement review;
14. audit closure.

Private information disclosed during engagement remains subject to its original authority, purpose, retention, and confidentiality controls.

# 16. Network Constitution

The institutional network may connect institutions, executives, boards, fintechs, CUSOs, vendors, regulators, investors, partners, associations, and service providers.

The network must not become:

- an ungoverned social graph;
- a pay-to-play vendor directory;
- an undisclosed lead-selling system;
- a public exposure mechanism for private institutional relationships;
- an engagement channel that bypasses institutional approval;
- a ranking system based primarily on commercial spend.

Relationship strength, relevance, trust, and access must remain separate concepts.

# 19. Constitutional State Machines

## 19.1 Principle Lifecycle

`draft → review → approved → active → amended → superseded → archived`

Rules:

- only approved principles may become active;
- active principles cannot be deleted;
- amendment creates a new version;
- superseded principles remain auditable;
- archival does not erase historical effect.

## 19.2 Exception Lifecycle

`requested → evidence_pending → under_review → approved | rejected → active → expiring → renewed | expired | revoked → closed`

## 19.3 Constitutional Conflict Lifecycle

`detected → triaged → interim_control → review → decision → remediation → validation → closed`

Critical conflicts require immediate suspension of the affected feature or workflow unless an authorized risk owner documents a lawful compensating control.

# 26. Constitutional Acceptance Tests

## Test 1 — Commercial Influence Cannot Purchase Verification

Given a paying customer requests a verified badge without required evidence, the request must remain unverified and produce a conflict record.

## Test 2 — Agent Cannot Commit Capital

Given an agent proposes an investment, the workflow may create a recommendation and evidence package but must stop before commitment until an authorized human approves.

## Test 3 — Private Information Cannot Become Public Silently

Given private tenant data enriches an internal profile, the public profile must remain unchanged unless an authorized publication or attestation workflow approves disclosure.

## Test 4 — Stale Information Remains Visible

Given a verified field exceeds its freshness threshold, the UI and API must return stale status and must not present it as current.

## Test 5 — Claimed Is Not Verified

Given an authorized representative claims a profile, identity and control may be verified while unsubstantiated company assertions remain attested or unverified.

## Test 6 — Matching Is Explainable

Given two eligible vendors are ranked, the system must return matched criteria, unmet criteria, evidence, conflicts, and commercial influence.

## Test 7 — Editorial Correction Is Auditable

Given a material factual error, the corrected content must preserve the previous version, correction reason, evidence, decision-maker, and publication timestamp.

## Test 8 — Constitutional Exception Expires

Given an approved exception reaches its end date, affected behavior must revert to the standard rule unless renewal is approved.

## Test 9 — Baseline Cannot Override Active Constitution

Given a historical document conflicts with the active charter, the active charter governs and the historical document remains noncanonical.

## Test 10 — Documentation Readiness Is Not Production Readiness

Given all documentation tests pass, the release status must not automatically assert that software, live integrations, penetration testing, migration, or production readiness is complete.

# 28. Constitutional Violation Response Runbook

## Trigger

A feature, actor, model, workflow, publication, or commercial process appears to violate an active constitutional principle.

## Owner

The owner is selected by violation class: editorial, product, security, privacy, commercial, tenant authority, or constitutional authority.

## Required Steps

1. Create violation record.
2. Preserve evidence and affected versions.
3. Classify severity and affected parties.
4. Apply immediate containment where necessary.
5. Suspend affected agent, route, connector, publication, or workflow if risk is critical.
6. Notify required authorities.
7. Determine whether users or institutions require notice.
8. Resolve conflict or revoke unauthorized behavior.
9. Correct affected data, publication, permissions, or workflow.
10. Validate remediation.
11. Record closure and lessons learned.
12. Determine whether a constitutional amendment, policy update, or new acceptance test is required.

## Closure Evidence

- root cause;
- affected artifacts;
- containment actions;
- decision authority;
- remediation;
- validation result;
- notifications;
- residual risk;
- closure approval.

# 29. Amendment Procedure

This charter may be amended only when:

- the proposed change is documented;
- affected product and institutional rights are identified;
- legal, compliance, privacy, security, editorial, commercial, and product impacts are reviewed as applicable;
- conflicts with existing principles are explicit;
- migration and backwards-compatibility effects are documented;
- acceptance tests are updated;
- an authorized constitutional authority approves;
- the new version is published to all affected implementation owners;
- the previous version remains preserved.

Emergency operational controls may suspend behavior immediately but may not permanently amend the charter without this process.
