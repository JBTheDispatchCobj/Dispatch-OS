# FS-15009 — Credit Union & Institution Innovation Journey
## From Institutional Understanding to Governed Innovation, Implementation, and Institutional Memory

**Section:** FS-15000 — The Auric, Named Terminal & Institutional Network Flywheel

**Authority:** Controlling doctrine for the institution-side journey from profile and strategic context through opportunity detection, evaluation, engagement, implementation, and verified outcome

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines the end-to-end innovation journey for credit unions, community banks, corporate credit unions, CUSOs, and other financial institutions operating through a Named Terminal.

The purpose of the journey is to replace fragmented innovation activity with a governed institutional process that connects:

- institutional identity
- strategy
- systems
- vendors
- risks
- regulation
- market intelligence
- readiness
- opportunity detection
- company discovery
- evidence
- approvals
- controlled engagement
- implementation
- measurement
- institutional memory

The governing doctrine is:

**An institution should not be asked to browse innovation. The Named Terminal should understand the institution well enough to identify what matters, explain why it matters, show which companies or partners fit, preserve the evidence required to act, and route the institution through a governed path from interest to outcome.**

The institution-side journey must reduce noise, improve decision quality, shorten institutional cycle time, and preserve knowledge after each engagement.

# 2. The Institutional Innovation Failure

Financial institutions often struggle with innovation because responsibility is fragmented.

A potential initiative may involve:

- CEO
- board
- CIO
- CISO
- compliance
- legal
- operations
- vendor management
- finance
- procurement
- business line
- project management
- third-party consultants
- implementation partners

The information required to evaluate an opportunity may be distributed across:

- strategic plans
- board minutes
- vendor inventories
- contracts
- policies
- system maps
- risk assessments
- emails
- budgets
- committee notes
- regulatory findings
- peer announcements
- product materials
- security evidence

Innovation then becomes dependent on:

- who knows the market
- who has the right relationship
- who remembers the last attempt
- who can assemble the documents
- who has time to move it forward
- who understands the approval path

The result is often one of two failures:

## 2.1 Innovation Theater

The institution attends events, hears pitches, launches committees, and discusses emerging technology without converting attention into controlled execution.

## 2.2 Uncontrolled Adoption

The institution adopts tools or vendors through fragmented departmental buying without complete evidence, governance, integration planning, or institutional memory.

The Named Terminal should prevent both.

# 11. Stage 8 — Opportunity Qualification

An opportunity should be qualified before market search or engagement.

Qualification should determine:

- Is the problem real?
- Is the problem material?
- Is there an institutional owner?
- Is the timing credible?
- Is there budget or a path to budget?
- Is there a relevant approval path?
- Is the institution ready enough?
- Is external capability required?
- Is there a measurable outcome?
- Are there disqualifying risks?
- Is the opportunity already addressed elsewhere?

Unqualified ideas may remain in discovery.

They should not be distributed as active institutional demand.

## 21. Canonical State Machine

The journey state machine is authoritative:

`PROFILE_INCOMPLETE → CONTEXT_READY → OPPORTUNITY_DETECTED → QUALIFICATION → READINESS_REVIEW → SPONSOR_PENDING → EVIDENCE_ASSEMBLY → GOVERNANCE_REVIEW → ENGAGEMENT_APPROVED → DILIGENCE → PILOT_DESIGN → PILOT_APPROVAL → PILOT_ACTIVE → PILOT_EVALUATION → IMPLEMENTATION_DECISION → IMPLEMENTATION_ACTIVE → OUTCOME_VERIFICATION → CLOSED_LEARNED`

Permitted terminal states are:

- `CLOSED_IMPLEMENTED`
- `CLOSED_REJECTED`
- `CLOSED_DEFERRED`
- `CLOSED_WITHDRAWN`
- `CLOSED_FAILED`
- `CLOSED_DUPLICATE`
- `CLOSED_OUT_OF_SCOPE`

No automated agent may transition a journey into `ENGAGEMENT_APPROVED`, `PILOT_APPROVAL`, `IMPLEMENTATION_DECISION`, or any closed state implying institutional authorization. Those transitions require an authenticated human approval object.

## 23. Decision Tables

### 23.1 Opportunity Qualification

| Condition | Decision |
|---|---|
| No strategic, regulatory, financial, operational, or member/customer relevance | Reject as noise |
| Relevant but insufficient evidence | Hold in `QUALIFICATION`; assign evidence request |
| Relevant and time-sensitive with incomplete data | Escalate with explicit uncertainty and temporary controls |
| Duplicate of active opportunity | Merge or link; do not create competing journey |
| Conflicts with policy or prohibited activity | Reject and preserve rationale |
| Meets value, urgency, and feasibility threshold | Advance to readiness review |

### 23.2 Readiness Routing

| Institution Readiness | Counterparty Readiness | Route |
|---|---|---|
| High | High | Standard diligence and pilot design |
| High | Medium | Counterparty remediation plan before pilot |
| Medium | High | Institution preparation workstream |
| Medium | Medium | Joint readiness plan; no production commitment |
| Low | Any | Defer or run discovery-only engagement |
| Any | Low | Reject, monitor, or require remediation evidence |

### 23.3 Governance Escalation

A journey MUST escalate when any of the following apply: regulated final decision, material customer/member data, new subprocessor, model use involving restricted data, contract above delegated threshold, unresolved high-risk finding, policy exception, board-reserved matter, capital commitment, investment decision, material public statement, or regulator-facing communication.

## 24. Scoring Models

The institution innovation score is advisory and MUST NOT replace human authority.

`Innovation Priority Score = 0.20 Strategic Alignment + 0.15 Member/Customer Impact + 0.15 Financial Impact + 0.10 Regulatory Urgency + 0.10 Operational Pain + 0.10 Readiness + 0.10 Evidence Quality + 0.10 Relationship Confidence`

All component scores are 0–100 and must expose source, timestamp, confidence, and missing-input flags.

Readiness dimensions:

- executive sponsorship
- business owner capacity
- budget availability
- policy readiness
- security readiness
- data readiness
- integration readiness
- vendor-management readiness
- implementation capacity
- measurement readiness

A score may not be displayed without its completeness percentage and last-assessed date.

## 29. Validation Rules

- Every state transition must be legal and auditable.

## 32. Acceptance Tests

1. A detected opportunity cannot advance without an owner.
2. A relationship with an executive does not create approval authority.
3. A low-readiness counterparty cannot enter production implementation.
4. An agent can draft but cannot approve an engagement, pilot, investment, or production deployment.
5. Missing evidence produces an explicit gap rather than fabricated completion.
6. Stale evidence remains marked stale through ranking and recommendation outputs.
7. A policy exception routes to the correct human authority.
8. A pilot cannot start without rollback and stop conditions.
9. Every material state transition emits a canonical event.
10. Closed journey knowledge updates the institution graph without overwriting source provenance.
11. Tenant A cannot view Tenant B journeys, evidence, search results, graph context, exports, or agent context.
12. A rejected opportunity preserves its rationale and can be reconsidered only through a new version.

## 33. Operational Runbook

### Daily

- review newly detected opportunities
- triage evidence and ownership gaps
- monitor overdue approvals and diligence findings
- inspect failed event delivery and workflow retries
- review stale context affecting active journeys

### Weekly

- pipeline review by sponsor and business owner
- readiness and evidence-gap review
- pilot metric and risk review
- duplicate and conflicting opportunity reconciliation
- institutional-memory quality review

### Incident Handling

On suspected unauthorized approval, cross-tenant disclosure, corrupted evidence, prohibited agent action, or material data-classification error: suspend affected workflow, preserve audit evidence, revoke active tokens where necessary, notify security and governance owners, assess scope, remediate, document closure, and require explicit restart approval.

## 34. Migration Guidance

Existing innovation spreadsheets, committee trackers, vendor pipelines, and pilot logs should be imported as historical source records first. Identity resolution, authority mapping, status normalization, and evidence classification must occur before records become active journeys. Unresolved records remain `MIGRATED_UNVERIFIED`; no imported approval is considered valid without authority reconciliation.
