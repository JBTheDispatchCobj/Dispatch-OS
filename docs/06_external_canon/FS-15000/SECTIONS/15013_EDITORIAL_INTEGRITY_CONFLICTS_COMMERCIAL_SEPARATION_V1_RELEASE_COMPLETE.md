# FS-15013 — Editorial Integrity, Conflicts & Commercial Separation
## Preserving Publication Authority While Operating a Commercial Institutional Network

**Section:** FS-15000 — The Auric, Named Terminal & Institutional Network Flywheel  
**Document:** 15013  
**Status:** V1 Release Complete
**Authority:** Controlling doctrine for editorial independence, commercial disclosure, conflict review, sponsored content, paid profile services, investments, referrals, partner relationships, corrections, disputes, and institutional trust  
**Applies to:** The Auric, Named Terminals, Auric Works, editorial teams, commercial teams, profile services, readiness services, implementation, sponsorship, advertising, investments, referrals, transaction services, partner programs, public profiles, matching, opportunity distribution, and governance

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines how Auric Works preserves the credibility of The Auric and the integrity of the Named Terminal network while also operating a commercial business.

Auric Works may earn revenue from:

- Named Terminal subscriptions
- implementation
- managed services
- profile completion
- readiness services
- evidence organization
- enterprise configuration
- partner programs
- sponsorship
- advertising
- events
- referrals
- transaction or execution services where legally permitted
- investments
- strategic partnerships
- data or intelligence services

Those activities create real or perceived conflicts.

The system must make those conflicts visible, governable, reviewable, and auditable.

The governing doctrine is:

**Commercial relationships may exist.  
Commercial influence must never be hidden.  
Editorial truth, profile verification, readiness, matching, eligibility, and institutional approval must remain governed by their own evidence and authority standards.**

---

# 2. Why This Doctrine Is Critical

The Auric is intended to become the publication of record for credit unions, CUSOs, fintechs, vendors, community banks, innovation, regulation, and cooperative markets.

At the same time, Auric Works intends to operate:

- institutional software
- readiness services
- profile services
- managed operating infrastructure
- partner programs
- commercial engagements
- network workflows
- implementation services
- possibly investment, referral, or transaction-related services where permitted

This creates a structural credibility risk.

The market may reasonably ask:

- Does payment influence coverage?
- Does a customer receive favorable treatment?
- Can a company buy a stronger profile?
- Can an advertiser affect rankings?
- Does an investment relationship affect analysis?
- Does a referral fee affect matching?
- Can a Named Terminal customer suppress negative reporting?
- Can Auric Works promote companies it financially benefits from?
- Can editorial content become disguised lead generation?
- Can commercial teams promise access or coverage?
- Can private customer information influence public reporting?

If these questions are not answered structurally, the publication-of-record ambition becomes fragile.

# 3. Governing Principles

## 3.1 Truth Is Not a Commercial Entitlement

No party may buy:

- favorable facts
- false verification
- removal of accurate history
- readiness approval
- hidden ranking advantage
- undisclosed recommendation
- guaranteed institutional selection
- suppression of material risk

## 3.2 Commercial Relationships Must Be Visible

Relevant commercial relationships must be recorded and disclosed to the appropriate reviewers and, where material, to readers or institutional decision-makers.

## 3.3 Editorial Authority Is Separate

Editorial decisions should be made by designated editorial authority using evidence, relevance, public interest, and publication standards.

## 3.4 Matching Is Separate

Matching and eligibility should use disclosed criteria, readiness, evidence, fit, and governance.

Editorial coverage alone should not create preferential ranking.

## 3.5 Profile Verification Is Separate

Paid profile services may improve completeness and organization.

They may not create false verification.

## 3.6 Institutional Approval Is Separate

No commercial relationship with Auric Works should bypass the institution’s evidence, risk, compliance, legal, procurement, board, or executive authority.

## 3.7 Private Data Stays Private

Private tenant data may not be used publicly without authority.

## 3.8 Conflicts Must Be Auditable

Conflict identification, review, mitigation, disclosure, and approval should be preserved as structured records.

# 4. Conflict Classes

The system should classify conflicts.

## 4.1 Customer Conflict

The subject is a current or prospective Named Terminal customer.

## 4.2 Sponsor or Advertiser Conflict

The subject pays for sponsorship, advertising, event participation, or promoted distribution.

## 4.3 Paid Profile Conflict

The subject pays for profile completion, readiness, evidence organization, or content-production services.

## 4.4 Implementation Conflict

Auric Works or a partner implements, operates, or supports the subject’s product or Terminal.

## 4.5 Investment Conflict

Auric Works, an affiliate, a partner, an employee, or a related vehicle has an investment or economic interest.

## 4.6 Referral Conflict

Auric Works or a partner may receive compensation from a referral.

## 4.7 Transaction Conflict

Auric Works or a partner may receive revenue from a completed investment, participation, procurement, implementation, placement, or transaction.

## 4.8 Strategic Partner Conflict

The subject is a strategic, distribution, data, or channel partner.

## 4.9 Personal Conflict

An employee, editor, reviewer, executive, or contractor has a material personal relationship.

## 4.10 Competitive Conflict

Auric Works or a related company competes directly with the subject.

## 4.11 Source Conflict

A source has a commercial, personal, or competitive interest in the story or decision.

## 4.12 Institutional Conflict

A Named Terminal operator has a commercial or governance interest in the subject.

# 5. Conflict Severity

Conflicts should be assessed by severity.

## 5.1 Low

Relationship exists but is unlikely to affect judgment.

Example:

- ordinary subscription
- minor event attendance
- broad membership relationship

## 5.2 Moderate

Relationship could create perceived influence.

Example:

- active advertiser
- paid profile service
- implementation discussion
- known personal relationship

## 5.3 High

Relationship could materially influence editorial, matching, or institutional decision-making.

Example:

- investment
- transaction economics
- strategic partnership
- active implementation
- referral compensation
- executive personal interest

## 5.4 Prohibitive

Conflict cannot be mitigated sufficiently.

Example:

- reviewer has direct financial control over the outcome
- source is fabricated or materially compromised
- commercial team has promised coverage or ranking
- private information would need to be misused
- required disclosure cannot be made

A prohibitive conflict may require:

- reassignment
- recusal
- independent review
- nonpublication
- suspension
- termination
- withdrawal from commercial role

# 7. Editorial Independence

Editorial independence means:

- commercial teams cannot guarantee coverage
- advertisers cannot approve editorial conclusions
- customers cannot remove accurate reporting
- investments do not guarantee positive treatment
- noncustomers are not punished through coverage
- subject refusal to buy does not affect editorial truth
- sponsored content is labeled
- commercial relationships are disclosed where material
- corrections remain evidence-based
- high-risk conflicts receive independent review

Editorial independence does not mean editorial teams ignore commercial context.

It means commercial context is visible and cannot silently control judgment.

# 37. Controlling Statement

Auric Works can operate a publication, a software platform, a network, readiness services, implementation, partnerships, investments, referrals, and commercial programs.

It can do so credibly only if the influence of those activities is visible and governed.

The Auric must remain more trustworthy than the commercial incentives surrounding it.

Named Terminal recommendations must remain more explainable than the relationships financing them.

Profiles must remain more accurate than the services used to complete them.

Institutional approval must remain more authoritative than Auric Works’ economic interest.

The system wins when the market can see the conflict, understand the mitigation, and still trust the record, the recommendation, and the process.


# Volume-Specific Implementation Artifacts


## Conflict Object Schema

Required fields: conflict ID, subject, relationship type, financial or nonfinancial interest, affected content/profile/match/engagement, severity, source, disclosure requirement, recusal requirement, information barrier, reviewer, mitigation, approval, effective period, status, and audit history.

## Commercial Relationship Registry

Relationship classes include customer, prospect, advertiser, sponsor, referral source, referral recipient, implementation client, partner, investor, portfolio company, vendor, data provider, affiliate, owner, lender, borrower, and compensated expert. Each record includes economic terms or a protected summary, start/end dates, affected entities, disclosure policy, and conflict-review status.

## Severity and Mitigation Matrix

Low conflicts require record and routine disclosure. Moderate conflicts require visible disclosure and independent review. High conflicts require recusal, information barriers, and approval by the conflict authority. Critical conflicts require suspension, reassignment, or prohibition. Concealment automatically raises severity.

## Editorial / Commercial Authority Matrix

Editorial controls story selection, sourcing, framing, correction, and publication. Commercial controls sales, sponsorship logistics, and customer relationships but cannot direct findings. Product controls features and methodology implementation but cannot override editorial truth. Conflict review may require disclosure, recusal, separation, or prohibition. Executive authority cannot silently waive these controls.

## Sponsored Content and Paid Profile Controls

Sponsored content requires explicit labeling, sponsor identity, scope, approval, and separation from independent editorial scoring. Paid profile services may assist a company in supplying information, organizing evidence, or completing readiness work; payment does not create verification, favorable ranking, eligibility, or suppression rights.

## Ranking Methodology Control

Every ranking or recommendation must identify purpose, eligible population, factors, weights or qualitative treatment, evidence freshness, missing-data handling, conflict treatment, commercial exclusions, review cadence, and appeal process. Commercial payment may not be an undisclosed factor.

## Gifts and Benefits Policy

Employees, contractors, reviewers, and agents may not accept benefits that could reasonably influence editorial, verification, ranking, matching, diligence, or approval. Thresholds, preapproval, disclosure, return, and recusal rules apply. Cash or cash equivalents are prohibited except approved compensation arrangements recorded in the commercial registry.

## APIs and Events

Core operations create, disclose, review, mitigate, approve, recuse, suspend, resolve, and audit conflicts. Events include `conflict.detected`, `disclosure.required`, `recusal.applied`, `information_barrier.applied`, `conflict.mitigated`, `content.corrected`, `ranking.recalculated`, and `violation.enforced`.

## Enforcement Runbook

A suspected violation is preserved, triaged, assigned independently, investigated against evidence, and resolved with correction, disclosure, recusal, retraining, suspension, contract remedy, termination, retraction, or public explanation as warranted. Commercial personnel may not close an editorial-integrity investigation involving their own account.

# Production Implementation Specification

## Canonical Record Requirements

Every object defined by this volume SHALL include a stable identifier, tenant or public-scope identifier, version, lifecycle state, provenance class, source references, confidence, freshness, owner, created and updated timestamps, review state, visibility policy, retention policy, and audit history. Public, private, synthetic, attested, inferred, and agent-generated records SHALL remain distinguishable in storage, retrieval, display, export, and downstream decision support.

Unknown, stale, disputed, incomplete, and unavailable values SHALL be represented explicitly. No implementation may coerce missing values into false, zero, approved, verified, or current values.

## Permission and Authority Controls

Roles are evaluated through FS-6000 authority records and FS-12000 IAM policy. Agents may retrieve, classify, compare, summarize, calculate, draft, recommend, route, monitor, and assemble evidence. Agents may not approve regulated or material actions, commit capital, bind an institution, publish a material disputed assertion without editorial approval, suppress a correction, waive policy, or disclose private tenant information.

Material actions require an Approval Object that records the action, evidence set, requested authority, approver identity, decision, conditions, timestamp, expiration, and audit reference.

## API Conventions

All APIs use versioned resource paths, idempotency keys for material writes, optimistic concurrency through record version or ETag, tenant and actor context, correlation IDs, explicit error objects, and audit-event emission. Typical operations include create, retrieve, list, update, submit, approve, reject, suspend, resume, close, dispute, correct, and export where permitted.

Every material mutation emits an event containing event ID, event type, aggregate ID, aggregate version, tenant or public scope, actor, causation ID, correlation ID, occurred-at timestamp, provenance class, and redacted payload appropriate to the subscriber.

## Validation Rules

1. IDs are unique within their canonical namespace.
2. State transitions must appear in the approved state machine.
3. Required evidence must be current and permitted for the stated use.
4. Approval authority must match the requested material action.
5. Public and private fields may not be merged into an indistinguishable record.
6. Attested claims do not become verified facts without the applicable verification rule.
7. Inferred or agent-generated conclusions must include method, confidence, and review state.
8. Stale records remain visible as stale until refreshed or retired.
9. Disputed records retain the original, dispute, resolution, and version history.
10. Commercial relationships and material conflicts must be disclosed where relevant.

## Observability and Analytics

The implementation SHALL record volume, state conversion, cycle time, error rate, approval latency, evidence completeness, freshness, dispute rate, correction rate, abandonment, successful outcome, and downstream reuse. Metrics must be segmented by tenant, role, object class, workflow class, source class, and channel only where privacy and contractual rules permit.

## Failure Handling

Failures are classified as validation, permission, evidence, dependency, integration, policy, conflict, timeout, duplicate, stale-data, or internal errors. Recoverable failures may retry under bounded policy. Material or repeated failures create an exception record, preserve evidence, notify the accountable owner, and route to human review. Compensation actions must never erase audit history.

## Acceptance Test Catalog

- create a valid canonical record and retrieve its complete lineage;
- reject a duplicate canonical ID;
- reject an invalid state transition;
- preserve unknown and stale values explicitly;
- prevent private fields from entering public output;
- prevent an agent from exercising material approval authority;
- require current evidence before a material action;
- record a human approval with conditions and expiration;
- disclose a material commercial conflict;
- support dispute, correction, versioning, and audit retrieval;
- emit the required events for every material transition;
- preserve tenant isolation in search, export, graph traversal, and agent context.

## Operations Runbook

The accountable product owner maintains registries, state machines, decision tables, permissions, templates, and metrics. Operations reviews failed and stalled records daily, stale or expiring evidence on the configured cadence, disputes and corrections within policy SLA, and material conflict or security events immediately. Changes require versioning, impact analysis, test evidence, approval, release notes, and rollback guidance.

## Migration Guidance

Baseline records are migrated by assigning canonical IDs, provenance classes, lifecycle states, visibility, source links, confidence, freshness, and owners. Ambiguous records enter a review queue. Legacy names are retained as aliases rather than silently rewritten. No record becomes verified, approved, or public merely because it existed in a prior system.

## Release Validation

The volume is content-complete when narrative doctrine, canonical objects, schemas, registries, state models, decision tables, permissions, evidence, approvals, APIs, events, fixtures, failure behavior, tests, runbooks, cross-section references, release manifest entries, and integrity records are present and internally consistent. Documentation completion does not certify software implementation or production readiness.
