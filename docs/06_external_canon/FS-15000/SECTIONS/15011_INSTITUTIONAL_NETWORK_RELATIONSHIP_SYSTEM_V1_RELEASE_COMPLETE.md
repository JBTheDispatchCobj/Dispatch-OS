# 1. Purpose

This document defines the institutional network and relationship system connecting participants across The Auric and Named Terminal ecosystem.

The network must not behave like generic social media.

Its purpose is not to maximize:

- followers
- comments
- impressions
- viral reach
- posting frequency
- cold outreach
- vanity influence

Its purpose is to make institutional relationships legible, permissioned, useful, and actionable.

The network should help participants understand:

- who knows whom
- who has worked together
- which affiliations are verified
- which relationships are current
- where warm paths exist
- who has relevant expertise
- which institutions are peers
- which companies have credible history
- who owns a relationship
- what engagement occurred
- what outcome followed
- which relationships are public, private, inferred, or disputed

The governing doctrine is:

**The network is a structured institutional relationship system.  
It exists to improve trust, context, discovery, matching, and governed execution.  
It must never confuse popularity with credibility or access with authority.**

# 36. Controlling Statement

The institutional network should make trust, affiliation, and relationship context more useful without making them less private or less accurate.

The Auric provides public context.

Profiles establish identity.

The relationship graph establishes institutional connection.

Named Terminals provide permissioned access and relevance.

Governed engagement converts relationships into action.

Dispatch OS preserves source, ownership, consent, confidence, and outcome.

The network wins when the right parties can find credible paths to one another—and when those paths improve institutional execution without becoming spam, influence theater, or pay-to-play access.

## 37.1 Invariants

1. No relationship may exist without at least two valid canonical parties.
2. A public relationship requires public evidence or affirmative publication consent.
3. An inferred relationship may never be presented as verified.
4. Relationship ownership identifies stewardship only; it grants no transaction, investment, procurement, lending, legal, compliance, or regulatory authority.
5. A disputed relationship may not be used for matching, ranking, or warm-path routing until resolved or explicitly permitted by policy.
6. Expired and terminated relationships remain historically queryable subject to retention and privacy policy.
7. Unknown, stale, inferred, attested, and verified states must remain visible in every projection.

# 39. Lifecycle and State Machines

## 39.1 Relationship Lifecycle

```text
PROPOSED
  -> UNVERIFIED
  -> SELF_ATTESTED
  -> COUNTERPARTY_ATTESTED
  -> EVIDENCE_VERIFIED
  -> INSTITUTION_VERIFIED
  -> ACTIVE
  -> AGING
  -> STALE
  -> EXPIRED | TERMINATED | REVOKED | DISPUTED
  -> ARCHIVED
```

Transitions SHALL require a recorded actor, timestamp, reason, policy decision, evidence delta, and prior/new state. No automated process may elevate a relationship to `institution_verified` without an authorized human or approved deterministic source.

## 39.2 Introduction Lifecycle

```text
DRAFT -> PURPOSE_REVIEWED -> PATH_IDENTIFIED -> CONSENT_REQUESTED
-> CONSENTED | DECLINED | EXPIRED
-> INTRODUCED -> ACKNOWLEDGED -> ENGAGED | NO_RESPONSE
-> OUTCOME_RECORDED -> CLOSED
```

## 39.3 Group Lifecycle

```text
PROPOSED -> CHARTERED -> INVITING -> ACTIVE -> PAUSED -> SUNSET -> ARCHIVED
```

## 39.4 Dispute Lifecycle

```text
OPENED -> TRIAGED -> EVIDENCE_REQUESTED -> UNDER_REVIEW
-> CORRECTED | RESTRICTED | REMOVED | UPHELD
-> APPEAL_WINDOW -> CLOSED
```

# 40. Relationship Strength and Trust Model

Relationship strength SHALL be explainable and shall not be a hidden social score.

## 40.1 Inputs

| Input | Weight Range | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| Verified affiliation | 0–25 | Evidence-backed formal relationship |
| Recency | 0–15 | Decays according to type |
| Interaction frequency | 0–10 | Capped to prevent activity gaming |
| Interaction depth | 0–15 | Project, committee, transaction, or implementation context |
| Mutual attestation | 0–10 | Both parties confirm |
| Outcome evidence | 0–15 | Verified positive or negative outcomes |
| Relationship duration | 0–5 | Log-scaled |
| Context relevance | 0–5 | Query-specific, not permanent reputation |

## 40.2 Penalties

- stale evidence: −5 to −25
- disputed relationship: route suppression
- revoked consent: route suppression
- undisclosed commercial interest: −20 and compliance review
- abuse or spam findings: −10 to full suspension
- inferred-only path: confidence cap of 0.60

## 40.3 Output

Every computed path SHALL return:

- score
- confidence
- contributing factors
- excluded factors
- freshness
- visibility constraints
- consent requirements
- commercial conflicts
- policy explanation

# 41. Warm-Path Decision Table

|---|---|

| Path includes private edge without consent | Suppress edge and recompute |
| Path includes disputed edge | Exclude edge |
| Path includes inferred edge | Allow discovery only; require verification before introduction |
| Path includes commercial sponsor | Display conflict and compensation disclosure |
| Relationship owner lacks sharing authority | Request owner approval |
| Target has blocked introductions | Stop |
| Purpose is vague or prohibited | Reject request |
| Regulated action is implied | Route to governed workflow with human authority |
| Multiple paths exist | Rank by verified relevance, not popularity |
| Path contains stale relationship | Warn and require confirmation |

# 44. Group, Committee, and Peer-Cohort Schema

A group object SHALL include:

- group ID and type
- charter and purpose
- sponsor and owner
- membership eligibility
- invitation and removal rules
- confidentiality class
- authority boundaries
- allowed content and activities
- meeting and event linkage
- retention rules
- conflicts and disclosures
- moderation and enforcement policy
- lifecycle status
- membership history

A peer cohort generated from data SHALL be labeled `derived`, include the criteria and refresh time, and SHALL NOT imply endorsement, membership, or consent.

# 45. Expertise and Reputation Evidence Model

Expertise is represented by evidence-backed expertise assertions, not a global reputation score.

Required fields:

- expertise assertion ID
- subject taxonomy ID
- person or institution ID
- evidence type and source
- evidence date
- verification state
- context and scope
- recency
- conflicts
- visibility
- confidence
- expiration/review date

Acceptable evidence includes verified roles, completed implementations, authored research, committee participation, licensed credentials, validated outcomes, training delivery, and cited public work. Paid sponsorship, self-description, follower count, impressions, and unverified endorsements may not independently establish expertise.

# 48. Feed and Search Ranking Rules

The network feed SHALL rank for institutional relevance, evidence quality, permission, recency, and user role. It SHALL NOT rank primarily by reactions, follower count, paid status, or controversy.

Search results SHALL expose:

- matched criteria
- relationship type
- verification and freshness
- path explanation
- visibility limitations
- conflicts and commercial disclosures
- missing or unknown criteria
- reason for ranking

Paid placements, sponsored participation, and commercial partnerships SHALL be labeled and separated from organic relationship relevance.

## 53.4 Incident Response

Owner: Network Operations Lead. Trigger: privacy leak, impersonation, systemic false affiliation, abuse surge, path disclosure defect, or cross-tenant access anomaly. Steps: contain, preserve evidence, disable affected capability, notify security/privacy, assess affected parties, remediate data and policy, validate recovery, communicate as required, document closure. No incident is closed without evidence and accountable human approval.

# 55. Analytics and Success Measures

The network SHALL measure institutional utility rather than engagement vanity.

Primary metrics:

- verified relationship coverage
- stale relationship rate
- consent completion time
- introduction acceptance rate
- introduction-to-governed-engagement conversion
- verified outcome rate
- dispute rate and resolution time
- false-affiliation incidence
- privacy/access-control incidents
- warm-path explanation acceptance
- peer-group participation quality
- cross-tenant denial correctness
- relationship evidence freshness
- paid/organic ranking separation compliance

No success metric may reward unsolicited outreach volume, follower accumulation, or unverified influence.

# 56. Migration Guidance

Legacy contacts, CRM associations, email-derived connections, and manually maintained spreadsheets SHALL enter as source records, not automatically verified relationships.

Migration stages:

1. inventory sources and permissions
2. classify source data as public, private, attested, inferred, synthetic, or agent-generated
3. normalize parties against FS-6000/FS-9000 identities
4. map relationship types to FS-5100
5. deduplicate candidates
6. assign confidence and freshness
7. request attestation where required
8. suppress private edges from public/network views
9. preserve source lineage and original values
10. validate tenant boundaries
11. obtain migration approval
12. activate production projections
