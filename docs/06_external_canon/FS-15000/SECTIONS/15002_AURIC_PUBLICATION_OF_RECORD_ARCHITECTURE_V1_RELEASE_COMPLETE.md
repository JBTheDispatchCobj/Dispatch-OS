## Public Intelligence, Editorial Authority, Institutional Memory, and Market Distribution

**Section:** FS-15000 — The Auric, Named Terminal & Institutional Network Flywheel  
**Document:** 15002  
**Status:** V1 Release Complete — Content-Complete Implementation Specification  
**Authority:** Controlling doctrine for The Auric as the public intelligence and publication-of-record layer  
**Applies to:** Editorial operations, intelligence production, publication formats, source governance, public profiles, distribution, corrections, sponsored content, market coverage, Terminal feeds, and Dispatch-powered content workflows

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines The Auric as the publication-of-record and public intelligence layer for credit unions, CUSOs, fintechs, vendors, community banks, financial-services innovation, institutional technology, regulation, capital, and executive leadership.

The Auric must operate as more than a media surface.

It must become the durable public record of the ecosystem and the primary public intelligence engine feeding the Named Terminal network.

The publication must combine:

- editorial judgment
- structured institutional intelligence
- market data
- evidence
- profiles
- timelines
- relationship context
- regulatory context
- vendor and product intelligence
- executive and board intelligence
- outcomes
- corrections
- distribution
- historical memory

The Auric should feel accessible, current, sharp, useful, and culturally literate.

Its authority should come from the fact that Dispatch OS maintains the underlying institutional graph, evidence, source lineage, profile history, and relationship context required to understand the market over time.

The core doctrine is:

**The Auric does not simply publish content about the ecosystem. It maintains the public operating record of the ecosystem and converts that record into structured intelligence for the Named Terminal network.**

- informed
- direct
- independent
- sharp
- accessible
- specific
- occasionally irreverent where appropriate
- evidence-backed
- commercially aware without being promotional
- serious about regulation and institutional risk

- empty corporate language
- excessive jargon
- false neutrality
- unsupported certainty
- breathless innovation language
- generic AI-generated tone
- promotional repetition
- overstated exclusivity
- hidden sponsorship
- personality without substance

- market analysis: evidence-heavy and interpretive
- profile feature: narrative and contextual
- regulatory brief: precise and restrained
- executive interview: conversational but substantive
- market map: analytical and structured
- industry commentary: opinionated and disclosed
- sponsored content: useful but clearly commercial

# 7. Canonical Editorial Product Types

The Auric should support distinct content products.

## 7.1 Breaking Alert

Fast, concise, source-backed notice of a material event.

Required characteristics:

- timestamp
- source
- affected entities
- what is confirmed
- what remains unknown
- correction path

## 7.2 Daily Brief

Curated summary of the most relevant developments.

May include:

- key events
- why they matter
- affected institutions
- links to profiles
- market implications
- Terminal-specific relevance

## 7.3 News Article

Straight reporting on an event or development.

## 7.4 Analysis

Explains implications, context, tradeoffs, and likely effects.

## 7.5 Profile Feature

Narrative and structured examination of an institution, company, executive, CUSO, product, or market actor.

## 7.6 Market Map

Structured view of a market category, participants, relationships, and positioning.

## 7.7 Ranking

Evidence-backed comparison using a disclosed methodology.

## 7.8 Interview

Executive, founder, regulator, investor, or operator conversation.

## 7.9 Regulatory Brief

Explains regulatory developments, applicability, and operational implications.

## 7.10 Data Story

Uses structured datasets, graphs, trends, or profiles to reveal market behavior.

## 7.11 Opinion

Clearly labeled viewpoint.

## 7.12 Sponsored Content

Clearly labeled commercial content subject to publication standards.

## 7.13 Company-Submitted Update

Verified company-supplied information that remains distinct from editorial reporting.

## 7.14 Terminal Brief

Institution-specific or persona-specific intelligence delivered privately within a Named Terminal.

## 7.15 Board Brief

Structured executive or board-ready summary.

- official institution filing

- official company statement
- verified executive statement
- government data
- direct interview

## Tier 2 — Strong Secondary

Examples:

- reputable publication
- industry database
- trade association
- established analyst report

## Tier 3 — Contextual or Unverified

Examples:

- social post
- anonymous tip
- conference remark
- informal relationship note

- source tier
- source date

- access date
- author

- corroboration
- dispute state
- permitted use

# 19. Editorial Product Quality Standard

A material Auric piece should answer:

1. What happened?
2. Who is involved?
3. What is confirmed?
4. What remains unknown?
5. Why does it matter?
6. Which institutions are affected?
7. What regulatory or operating context applies?
8. What changed from the prior state?
9. What evidence supports the piece?
10. What should the relevant reader watch next?

Not every format requires equal depth.

The principle is that the content should create useful institutional understanding.

# 23. What Must Never Happen

The Auric must never:

- publish invented sources
- convert speculation into fact
- hide commercial relationships
- sell undisclosed favorable coverage
- allow profile claimants to rewrite independent reporting
- allow sponsorship to silently control ranking
- use private tenant information publicly without authority
- allow agents to publish material allegations independently
- delete accurate history because a customer objects
- retain false information after credible correction evidence
- optimize volume at the expense of trust
- treat attention as the only measure of success

# 24. Acceptance Criteria

The Auric publication architecture is satisfied when:

1. The publication has defined editorial products and desks.
2. Every material content object has a source-backed lifecycle.
3. Public content enriches profiles and graph intelligence.
4. Named Terminal feeds receive institution-specific relevance.
5. AI supports but does not control editorial authority.
6. Sponsored, submitted, editorial, and verified-profile content are distinct.
7. Corrections propagate across public and Terminal surfaces.
8. Commercial relationships are disclosed and auditable.
9. Traffic, intelligence, profile, trust, and conversion outcomes are measured separately.
10. The Auric can publish across multiple channels from one canonical object.
11. Public and private institutional context remain separated.
12. The publication creates useful market understanding rather than generic volume.
13. The architecture supports publication-of-record durability and historical memory.
14. Every published entity can connect to a profile, relationship, timeline, or market-theme object where applicable.

# 27. Controlling Statement

The Auric must become the publication of record because it combines editorial judgment with Dispatch-powered institutional intelligence.

Its public content must improve the market’s shared understanding.

Its structured record must improve profiles, relationships, search, readiness, matching, and institutional action.

Its credibility must remain stronger than its commercial incentives.

Its intelligence must remain more durable than its article traffic.

# 30. Publication State Machine

```text
SIGNAL_DETECTED
  -> TRIAGED
  -> RESEARCH_OPEN
  -> EVIDENCE_READY
  -> DRAFTING
  -> FACT_REVIEW
  -> EDITORIAL_REVIEW
  -> CONFLICT_REVIEW (conditional)
  -> LEGAL_COMPLIANCE_REVIEW (conditional)
  -> APPROVED
  -> SCHEDULED | PUBLISHED
  -> MONITORING
  -> UPDATED | CORRECTED | SUPERSEDED | RETRACTED | ARCHIVED
```

## 30.1 Transition rules

| From | To | Required actor | Required evidence/condition |
|---|---|---|---|
| `SIGNAL_DETECTED` | `TRIAGED` | editor or triage agent + human confirmation | source exists, entity match attempted |
| `TRIAGED` | `RESEARCH_OPEN` | assigning editor | desk, owner, priority assigned |
| `RESEARCH_OPEN` | `EVIDENCE_READY` | reporter/researcher | minimum source threshold met |
| `EVIDENCE_READY` | `DRAFTING` | assigned author | claim ledger created |
| `DRAFTING` | `FACT_REVIEW` | author | all material claims linked to evidence |
| `FACT_REVIEW` | `EDITORIAL_REVIEW` | fact reviewer | unresolved factual exceptions = 0 or explicitly disclosed |
| `EDITORIAL_REVIEW` | `APPROVED` | authorized editor | editorial checklist passes |
| any prepublication | `CONFLICT_REVIEW` | system or editor | commercial/personal/portfolio conflict detected |
| any prepublication | `LEGAL_COMPLIANCE_REVIEW` | editor | high-risk allegations, regulated action, embargo, litigation, privacy |
| `APPROVED` | `PUBLISHED` | publisher service | channel contract valid; embargo expired |
| `PUBLISHED` | `CORRECTED` | corrections editor | correction record approved |
| `PUBLISHED` | `RETRACTED` | editor-in-chief + legal where applicable | material unreliability or prohibited publication |

No agent may independently move an item to `APPROVED`, `PUBLISHED`, `RETRACTED`, or close a material dispute.

# 31. Editorial Workflow Definitions

## 31.1 Breaking alert workflow

- Trigger: verified material event with urgency under 60 minutes.
- Minimum sources: one Tier 1, or two independent Tier 2 sources with explicit qualification.
- Required outputs: alert, source note, entity links, uncertainty statement, monitoring task.
- Approval: duty editor.
- SLA: target 15 minutes after evidence threshold.
- Post-publication: mandatory follow-up review within 4 hours.

## 31.2 Standard article workflow

- Trigger: approved research matter.
- Minimum sources: one authoritative source plus corroboration where material.
- Required claim ledger: every material factual assertion.
- Approval: desk editor; legal/compliance when rule triggers fire.
- Outputs: web article, Terminal feed event, profile update proposals, metadata package.

## 31.3 Regulatory brief workflow

- Requires authoritative text, effective date, jurisdiction, affected-entity mapping, obligations, uncertainty, and implementation implications.
- AI may summarize but may not issue a final legal or compliance conclusion.
- Approval: regulatory desk editor and designated compliance reviewer.

## 31.4 Sponsored content workflow

- Commercial request is opened outside the editorial queue.
- Sponsorship disclosure is mandatory in every channel variant.
- Editorial staff may reject misleading claims.
- Sponsor has no right to alter independent coverage, rankings, profile facts, or correction decisions.

## 31.5 Correction workflow

- Intake creates a `correction_record` and freezes deletion of the challenged version.
- Reviewer compares claim, source, publication state, and requested remedy.
- Material corrections retain visible history.
- Propagation updates web, email archive, Terminal feed, profile timeline, and syndication endpoints.

# 32. Decision Tables

## 32.1 Source sufficiency

| Materiality | Tier 1 available | Tier 2 count | Publish rule |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| low | no | 1 | May publish as attributed brief |
| moderate | no | 2 | May publish with uncertainty disclosure |
| high | yes | any | Publish after fact review |
| high | no | 2+ independent | Editor-in-chief approval required |

## 32.2 Conflict routing

|---|---|

| Sponsor is subject of coverage | sponsored team separated; no sponsor approval right |
| Portfolio/investment exposure | disclose and assign reviewer without economic interest |
| Personal relationship | recusal or documented supervisory approval |

# 33. Scoring Models

## 33.1 Source confidence score

`confidence = authority_weight × corroboration_factor × freshness_factor × directness_factor × consistency_factor`

All components range from 0.00 to 1.00. The stored score MUST retain component values and calculation version.

## 33.2 Publication authority score

Weighted components:

- evidence completeness: 30%
- source authority: 25%
- entity resolution confidence: 15%
- review completion: 15%
- correction history: 5%
- freshness: 10%

Score thresholds:

- 0–49: draft/internal only
- 50–69: publish only with material qualification
- 70–84: standard publication
- 85–100: publication-of-record eligible

Scores support review; they do not replace editorial judgment.

# 37. Validation Rules

A publication item is invalid when any of the following is true:

- required material claims lack evidence links
- source tier is absent
- public/private provenance is flattened
- an inferred fact is presented as verified
- stale data is presented without freshness disclosure
- sponsorship exists without visible disclosure
- a named subject lacks entity resolution or an explicit unresolved marker
- correction history is removed
- a prohibited agent authority is attempted
- an embargoed item is distributed early
- a regulated material conclusion lacks required human review
- a Terminal-only fact appears in a public variant without authorization
