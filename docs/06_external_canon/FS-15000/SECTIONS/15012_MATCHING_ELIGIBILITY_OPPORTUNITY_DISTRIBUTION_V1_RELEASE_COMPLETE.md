## Explainable Institutional Fit, Governed Access, and Neutral Market Allocation

**Section:** FS-15000 — The Auric, Named Terminal & Institutional Network Flywheel  
**Document:** 15012  
**Status:** V1 Release Complete — Content-Complete Implementation Specification  
**Authority:** Controlling doctrine for matching, eligibility, opportunity creation, opportunity distribution, prioritization, neutrality, sponsored visibility, and appeals  
**Applies to:** Credit unions, community banks, CUSOs, fintechs, vendors, partners, investors, opportunities, profiles, readiness, relationship paths, public intelligence, Named Terminals, governed engagement, and commercialization

---

# 1. Purpose

This document defines how Dispatch OS determines whether two or more market participants are relevant to one another, whether they are eligible to engage, how opportunities are created and distributed, and how ranking remains explainable, governed, and commercially neutral.

The system must distinguish among:

- discoverability
- relevance
- matching
- eligibility
- prioritization
- distribution
- introduction
- selection
- approval
- execution

These are not interchangeable.

A company may be relevant but not eligible.

A company may be eligible but not a priority.

A company may be highly ranked but not selected.

An institution may express interest but lack readiness.

A relationship path may exist but not authorize contact.

A paid participant may receive clearly labeled promotional visibility without receiving hidden algorithmic advantage.

The governing doctrine is:

**Matching identifies institutional fit.  
Eligibility determines whether engagement is permitted and sufficiently ready.  
Opportunity distribution determines who should see and act on the opportunity.  
Selection and approval remain governed human decisions.**

---

# 2. Why Matching Requires Governance

Institutional matching is more consequential than ordinary recommendation.

A recommendation may influence:

- vendor selection
- pilot activity
- procurement
- referrals
- investments
- partnerships
- implementation
- data access
- risk exposure
- member or customer outcomes
- institutional reputation

A matching system that optimizes only for:

- clicks
- response probability
- sponsorship
- popularity
- company growth
- profile completeness
- relationship proximity

would be institutionally unsafe.

Matching must account for:

- institutional need
- company capability
- systems
- regulation
- risk
- readiness
- evidence
- implementation capacity
- timing
- sponsor strength
- relationship path
- commercial conflicts
- prior outcomes
- explicit exclusions

The system should explain both positive and negative factors.

# 3. Core Definitions

## 3.1 Discoverability

Whether a participant, product, institution, or opportunity can appear in search or browsing.

## 3.2 Relevance

Whether there is a reasonable relationship between a participant’s need, capability, context, or role.

## 3.3 Match

A structured assessment that two or more parties may fit a defined use case or opportunity.

## 3.4 Eligibility

Whether a party is permitted and sufficiently prepared to participate in a specific engagement type.

## 3.5 Opportunity

A structured institutional need, gap, initiative, transaction, or relationship path with defined value, urgency, ownership, readiness, and next action.

## 3.6 Distribution

The process by which an opportunity is surfaced to eligible participants, owners, sponsors, or reviewers.

## 3.7 Promotion

Paid or sponsored visibility that remains labeled and separate from organic relevance.

## 3.8 Selection

A human institutional decision to advance a participant.

## 3.9 Approval

A governed authorization to proceed with a material action.

# 7. Opportunity Qualification

Before distribution, an opportunity should be qualified.

Qualification should determine:

- Is the problem real?
- Is the opportunity material?
- Is there an owner?
- Is there a sponsor?
- Is timing credible?
- Is there a budget or path to budget?
- Is an external solution required?
- Is there a measurable desired outcome?
- Is the institution ready enough?
- Are there legal, regulatory, or policy constraints?
- Is the opportunity already being addressed?
- Is external distribution permitted?

An opportunity may remain:

- signal
- discovery
- unqualified
- qualified
- restricted
- active
- paused
- closed

Unqualified signals should not be distributed as active institutional demand.

# 12. Matching Score Doctrine

The system may calculate a composite match score, but the score must not become the only decision input.

A score should be decomposable.

Example dimensions:

- need fit
- product fit
- institutional fit
- technology fit
- regulatory fit
- readiness
- evidence
- implementation
- commercial fit
- relationship path
- outcome history
- timing
- confidence

Each dimension should include:

- score
- weight
- evidence
- confidence
- freshness
- unknown state
- disqualifier
- explanation

Weights may vary by opportunity type.

# 13. Hard Gates Versus Weighted Factors

Some conditions should be hard gates.

Examples:

- prohibited data use
- missing legal eligibility
- failed identity verification
- critical security failure
- licensing requirement
- no consent
- explicit institution exclusion
- unresolved fraud
- unavailable implementation capacity
- conflict that cannot be mitigated

Other conditions may be weighted.

Examples:

- geography
- relationship path
- pricing preference
- peer adoption
- product maturity
- support model
- implementation speed

A high weighted score must not override a hard gate.

# 17. Opportunity Distribution Models

Opportunity distribution may use different models.

## 17.1 Direct Distribution

Opportunity sent to one or more named eligible participants.

## 17.2 Curated Distribution

Opportunity reviewed and distributed by Auric Works, a partner, or the institution.

## 17.3 Network Distribution

Opportunity made visible to an approved network cohort.

## 17.4 Invitation-Only

## 17.5 Open Qualified Distribution

Distribution model should be explicit.

# 24. Editorial Independence

Auric coverage should not automatically improve match score.

Coverage may create new evidence or public profile information.

The resulting facts may affect relevance.

The editorial act itself should not create preferential ranking.

Likewise:

- negative coverage should not automatically disqualify
- sponsored content should not create eligibility
- lack of coverage should not imply weakness

The system should use evidence, not publication favor.

This specification is satisfied when:

1. Relevance, match, eligibility, distribution, selection, and approval are distinct.
2. Opportunities exist as canonical objects.
3. Hard gates cannot be overridden by high scores.
4. Match dimensions are explainable.
5. Unknown and stale data remain visible.
6. Eligibility is engagement-specific.
7. Distribution scope and model are explicit.

## 36.4 Eligibility State Machine

```text
NOT_ASSESSED
  -> IDENTITY_REVIEW
  -> PRODUCT_REVIEW
  -> EVIDENCE_REVIEW
  -> READINESS_REVIEW
  -> COMPLIANCE_SECURITY_REVIEW
  -> CAPACITY_REVIEW
  -> COMMERCIAL_REVIEW
  -> CONSENT_REVIEW
  -> CONFLICT_REVIEW
  -> ELIGIBLE | CONDITIONALLY_ELIGIBLE | INELIGIBLE | INSUFFICIENT_INFORMATION | REVIEW_REQUIRED
```

Allowed transitions:

| Current State | Event | Next State | Required Authority |
|---|---|---|---|
| not_assessed | assessment_started | identity_review | system or analyst |
| any review state | evidence_missing | insufficient_information | system or analyst |
| any review state | material_conflict_detected | review_required | conflict reviewer |
| conflict_review | all_gates_passed | eligible | eligibility reviewer |
| conflict_review | remediable_condition | conditionally_eligible | eligibility reviewer |
| conflict_review | prohibiting_condition | ineligible | authorized human reviewer |
| conditionally_eligible | condition_satisfied | eligible | authorized human reviewer |
| any final state | material_new_evidence | not_assessed | system with audit event |

Automated logic may propose a gate outcome. It may not finally waive policy, approve a regulated counterparty, commit capital, approve a vendor, or authorize a material institutional action.

| expired | expiration policy applied | refresh or archive | requalify |

| Gate | Required Inputs | Pass | Conditional | Fail | Unknown Handling |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Identity | legal identity, ownership, claimed-profile status | verified | pending minor verification | disputed/blocked identity | insufficient_information |
| Product | capability, product definition, supported use case | capability demonstrated | roadmap-dependent | no relevant product | insufficient_information |
| Evidence | required evidence package | current and sufficient | remediable gap | materially deficient/false | hold distribution |
| Readiness | implementation, support, governance readiness | threshold met | improvement plan approved | below minimum | review_required |
| Compliance/Security | applicable controls and reviews | approved/current | scoped condition | prohibited/high-risk unresolved | ineligible |
| Capacity | implementation and servicing capacity | available | scheduled capacity | unavailable | conditionally_eligible |
| Commercial | pricing and contracting clarity | clear | negotiation required | material opacity/conflict | review_required |
| Consent | contact and distribution rights | explicit | limited scope | denied | ineligible |
| Conflict | editorial/commercial/relationship conflicts | none or disclosed | mitigation approved | unmitigable | ineligible |

## 36.7 Scoring Model

The default V1 score is a transparent weighted model applied only after hard gates permit scoring.

```text
overall_score = Σ(weight_i × normalized_factor_i) / Σ(active_weight_i)
confidence = evidence_coverage × freshness_factor × source_reliability
```

Default factors:

| Factor | Default Weight | Hard Gate? | Null Treatment |
|---|---:|---|---|
| need/use-case fit | 20 | no | excluded from denominator and disclosed |
| institutional fit | 10 | no | excluded and disclosed |
| technology fit | 12 | sometimes | review_required when mandatory system is unknown |
| regulatory/policy fit | 12 | yes when prohibited | cannot be imputed |
| security/data fit | 12 | yes | cannot be imputed |
| readiness fit | 12 | threshold | unknown lowers confidence |
| commercial fit | 8 | no | unknown lowers confidence |
| relationship fit | 4 | no | zero advantage if unknown |
| timing/capacity fit | 5 | threshold | conditionally_eligible |
| outcome/reputation fit | 5 | no | source-weighted |

Rules:

1. Sponsorship never modifies organic score or organic rank.
2. Relationship proximity may improve path confidence but may not overcome eligibility failure.
3. Missing data is not scored as failure unless a published rule requires evidence.
4. Inferred data must be visibly labeled and cannot satisfy a hard evidence gate by itself.
5. Negative evidence must identify source, date, severity, review status, and appeal rights.
6. Score versions are immutable after use in a distribution decision.
7. Material weight changes require governance approval, regression testing, and release notes.

## 36.9 Distribution Sequencing Rules

Distribution sequencing must be deterministic and auditable.

1. Apply confidentiality and legal restrictions.
2. Resolve hard exclusions.
3. Evaluate eligibility.
4. Calculate organic relevance among eligible candidates.
5. Apply capacity and timing constraints.
6. Apply diversification or concentration rules when approved.
7. Identify relationship paths without changing organic rank.
8. Render clearly labeled sponsored placements separately.
9. Obtain required human distribution approval.
10. Issue invitations and preserve recipient-level audit events.

Tie-breaking order:

1. higher evidence confidence
2. higher readiness confidence
3. fewer unresolved conditions
4. lower implementation mismatch
5. earlier complete response
6. deterministic hash lottery when all preceding factors are equal

## 36.11 API Contracts

### Create Opportunity

`POST /v1/opportunities`

Required permission: `opportunity.create`

Response: `201 Created` with immutable opportunity ID, version, and audit event.

### Qualify Opportunity

`POST /v1/opportunities/{opportunity_id}/qualification`

Returns gate-level results, missing evidence, conflicts, and recommended next state. A final qualified state requires the approval ID specified by policy.

### Generate Matches

`POST /v1/opportunities/{opportunity_id}/matches:generate`

Input includes score version, candidate scope, allowed data classes, and cutoff timestamp. Output includes eligible, conditional, review-required, and excluded candidates with explanations.

### Approve Distribution

`POST /v1/opportunities/{opportunity_id}/distribution:approve`

Requires human approval token, distribution plan hash, recipient-set hash, and conflict certification.

### Submit Response

`POST /v1/distributions/{distribution_id}/responses`

Validates recipient identity, deadline, response schema, conflicts, and evidence attachments.

### File Appeal

`POST /v1/matches/{match_id}/appeals`

Creates a non-destructive appeal record and freezes the contested explanation version.

All mutation APIs require tenant context, idempotency key, actor identity, reason, and audit metadata.

## 36.13 Explanation Contract

Every surfaced match must display:

- eligibility status
- organic score and score version
- confidence
- top positive factors
- top negative factors
- unknown or stale factors

A user must be able to understand why a candidate was included, excluded, or ranked differently without exposing restricted data.

## 36.14 Sponsored Visibility Controls

Sponsored visibility is a commercial surface, not a scoring input.

Required controls:

- persistent `Sponsored` label
- separate sponsored-placement ID
- campaign and payer disclosure available to authorized users
- no mutation of organic match score
- no suppression of higher-ranked organic results
- no eligibility override
- frequency cap
- conflict review
- audience-consent enforcement
- independent editorial separation under FS-15013
- audit trail of placement, impression, click, and engagement

## 36.15 Privacy, Classification, and Confidentiality

Opportunity data must preserve the following dimensions independently:

- source class: public, private, synthetic, attested, inferred, agent-generated
- sensitivity: public, internal, confidential, restricted
- visibility: owner-only, named group, tenant, partner cohort, network, public
- freshness: current, aging, stale, unknown
- consent: none, limited, explicit, withdrawn
- purpose: discovery, qualification, diligence, distribution, selection, measurement

Restricted opportunity details may be used for matching only when policy, consent, and purpose allow. Explanations must redact prohibited attributes while still stating that a restricted factor affected eligibility when legally permissible.

## 36.16 Bias, Neutrality, and Fairness Controls

The matching system must be tested for:

- sponsorship leakage into organic ranking
- popularity bias
- relationship-network bias
- incumbent bias
- profile-completeness bias
- size bias
- geography bias
- data-availability bias
- selective exclusion
- feedback-loop amplification
- proxy use of prohibited attributes

Neutrality reports should compare exposure, eligibility, rank, response, selection, and outcome rates by approved cohort. Differences trigger review; they do not automatically imply wrongdoing.

# 37. Controlling Statement

The matching system should make institutional relevance visible without pretending relevance is authority.

Dispatch OS should identify fit, eligibility, risk, evidence, readiness, timing, and relationship context.

The Named Terminal should explain why an opportunity or participant is relevant.

Governed engagement should control what happens next.

Human institutions should still select, approve, and execute.

The system wins when better-qualified parties find one another faster, commercial influence remains visible, and every outcome makes future matching more accurate and more trustworthy.
